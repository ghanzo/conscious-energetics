## Part I: Creation
*What energy is and where we stand.*

### Chapter 1: Energy
*What energy actually is — beyond the commodity, beyond the abstraction.*

- The word and its origins (energeia)
- Energy as physics defines it, as the body knows it, as traditions feel it
- Why all three views matter and none is complete alone
- Energy must be gathered, stored, transformed — it is finite and constrained
- The three dimensions: what caused this moment (past), what's happening now (present), what could happen next (future)
- The reader leaves with: a felt sense that energy is the most fundamental thing, and a framework for seeing it everywhere

*Scaffolding: Cause/Choice/Change introduced experientially. Informational/Kinetic/Potential. This chapter IS the Cause chapter within Part I.*

### Chapter 2: The Ledger
*How much energy we have, how we got it, and why the math is turning against us.*

- The carbon pulse: civilization is burning inheritance, not income (Hagens)
- GDP and energy: the correlation that economics ignores (Keen)
- EROEI: from 100:1 gushers to 3:1 tar sands
- Ore grades declining, grinding costs rising (Michaux, comminution laws)
- We don't run out — we run out of surplus
- The resource pyramid: lots of oil left, but most of it is bad oil
- The reader leaves with: a clear understanding that the energy crisis is not about scarcity but about net returns

*Scaffolding: This is the Choice chapter — the ledger reveals the branching possibilities and constraints. Manifestations (Content/Context/Transformation) operate here implicitly.*

### Chapter 3: The Stage
*The planet we live on — volatile, ancient, and not as stable as we think.*

- Earth is a forge, not a garden (Carlson, catastrophism)
- Cosmic cycles: Milankovitch, Younger Dryas, impact events
- The cosmic backstory in brief: Big Bang → stars → elements → planets → life
- Conservation, dissipation, emergence — how energy behaves at every scale
- Vulnerability demands adaptation, not arrogance
- The reader leaves with: humility about our position and a sense of the forces that shaped this moment

*Scaffolding: This is the Change chapter — transformation at cosmic and planetary scales. Behaviors (Conservation/Dissipation/Emergence) operate here.*

## Part II: Interaction
*How energy flows through life, civilization, and power — and the choices before us.*

### Chapter 4: The Flame
*Life as an energy system — from the first cell to the forest canopy.*

- Metabolism: life's pilot flame, the thing that must never go out
- Photosynthesis: the great alchemy — sunlight into sugar into everything
- Food webs, trophic levels, the biomass inventory
- Fossil fuels as "delayed reverb" — ancient sunlight sealed underground
- Reverberation: how complex ecosystems recycle energy and build resilience
- More complexity = more stability = more life
- The reader leaves with: a view of the biosphere as a living energy machine, not a backdrop

*Scaffolding: Survival chapter within Interaction. The biological Cause — what sustains existence.*

### Chapter 5: The Fire
*Humanity's relationship with energy — from the first hearth to the oil age.*

- Fire as the first great leap: cooking, warmth, metallurgy, social bonding
- Wood and its limits: Rome, the Maya, deforestation crises
- Coal and the industrial revolution: steam, steel, urbanization
- Oil and modernity: internal combustion, petrochemicals, globalization
- Nuclear: the road not fully taken
- Each transition: more power, more people, more cost
- The reader leaves with: the human energy story as one continuous arc, each leap built on the last

*Scaffolding: Social chapter within Interaction. The relational Choice — how societies organize around energy.*

### Chapter 6: The Board
*The geopolitical game of energy — who controls it, who's losing it, what comes next.*

- The petrodollar system: how oil became the dollar became empire
- Iran, Venezuela, Ukraine as fronts in a quiet energy war
- China's counter-move: decoupling from oil, securing minerals, building infrastructure
- The network vs. the state: technology forcing a fork (Balaji)
- Imperial cycles: the changing world order as energy density wanes (Dalio)
- AI as accelerant: the pressure cooker that speeds everything up
- The ecosystem as substrate: we are biological, no technology changes that
- Risks of synthetic substitution: microplastics, PFAS, fertility
- The generative vision: stewardship, not supplanting
- The reader leaves with: a clear-eyed view of where the world actually stands and what's at stake

*Scaffolding: Sexual chapter within Interaction. The generative Change — what future is being born from this conflict.*

## Part III: Existence
*What must change in us — consciousness, agency, and the horizon.*

### Chapter 7: The Arrow
*The evolution of consciousness — where we are developmentally and where we need to go.*

- Pluralism: the stage where truth is relative and action is paralyzed
- Integral: the stage where truth is probabilistic and action is aligned (Wilber)
- The metacrisis: why our problems are systemic and require higher-order thinking (Schmachtenberger)
- Development is not optional — it's the software update the species needs
- The reader leaves with: a map of where humanity is stuck and what "growing up" actually means

*Scaffolding: Being chapter within Existence. The Cause of the inner world — what already exists in consciousness.*

### Chapter 8: The Work
*The individual path — what you can actually do with your own nervous system.*

- Enlightenment is not mystical — it's biological and accessible (Jeff Martin)
- Internal technology: the nervous system as upgradeable hardware
- Biological selfhood: cells as computational, self-organizing systems (Levin)
- The free energy principle: living systems minimize surprise to stay coherent (Friston)
- Consciousness as fundamental, not emergent (Spira, Tolle)
- The reader leaves with: a sense that inner transformation is real, practical, and available

*Scaffolding: Doing chapter within Existence. The Choice to act on oneself — agency turned inward.*

### Chapter 9: Omega
*The horizon — what conscious energetics points toward.*

- The ontological shock: non-human intelligence as proof of concept
- If higher intelligence exists, conscious energetics (mind shaping matter) is possible
- Reality as self-configuring language (Langan, CTMU)
- The deeper architecture of reality (Landry, immanent metaphysics)
- The full arc: from cosmic dust to conscious participation
- Energy is not just the substrate — it is what we are becoming
- The reader leaves with: awe, orientation, and a sense of what's possible

*Scaffolding: Becoming chapter within Existence. The Change that is the future — what energy becomes when consciousness is fully realized.*
