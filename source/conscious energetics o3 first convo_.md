Sun While many of Earth‚Äôs earliest cells relied on chemical energy from their surroundings, a

sunlight to build energy-rich compounds (notably glucose) from carbon dioxide and water. In

secondary consumers (carnivores), tertiary consumers, and so on. In parallel, decomposers like 
fungi and bacteria break down dead material, returning nutrients to the soil or water, closing 
loops in the nutrient cycle. Yet while matter recycles, energy flow is typically 
one-way‚Äîeventually dissipating as heat. The input that keeps it all going is, once again, 
sunlight captured by photosynthesis. Ecosystem Complexity and Evolution With photosynthesis
