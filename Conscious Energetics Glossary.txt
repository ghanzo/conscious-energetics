Conscious Energetics Glossary


Afflux/Eflux:
Paired concepts describing the inward and outward flow of energy, information, or influence.

    Afflux: The inflow or absorption of energy and information into a system.
    Eflux: The outflow or emission of energy and information from a system.
    Together, they represent dynamic processes of exchange and transformation in any energetic or informational environment.

Axioms (of Energy):
Foundational metaphysical principles framing energy as the underlying cause, driver of change, and enabler of choice. These axioms set the conceptual structure for understanding how energy shapes existence.

Cause:
The principle that energy is the origin of effects, ensuring continuity from past to present and providing context for how events unfold. It anchors phenomena within a relational chain.

Change:
The dynamic quality of energy that enables transformation in form, state, or information. Change underlies experience, growth, and evolution, making time and motion perceptible.

Choice:
A dimension of energy’s agency, representing the potential for selecting among possibilities. It emphasizes that energy is not merely passive but affords meaningful options and pathways.

Choice/Change/Cause (as a Triple):
A foundational triad that frames the metaphysics of energy:

    Choice: The potential and agency inherent in energy to select among options.
    Change: The dynamic quality that transforms forms, states, and experiences.
    Cause: The principle that events arise from underlying energies, providing continuity and coherence.
    This triple underpins the narrative that energy is both shaped by and shaping reality through directed action, evolution, and interconnectedness.

Consciousness:
The experiential reality through which energy becomes known. At its broadest level, it may be seen as absolute and universal, while at the personal level, it is the witnessing, interpreting, and experiencing of energy’s forms and movements.

Conservation (1st Law):
A principle stating that the total amount of energy remains constant; it cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed. This concept ensures the continuity and persistence of energy through time and space.

Cosmogony:
The study or narrative of the universe’s origins and evolution. In this context, it addresses how energy and informational patterns guided the cosmos from the Big Bang through its unfolding complexity.

Emergence (4th Law):
The principle that from simpler, lower-level interactions, novel and more complex structures and patterns spontaneously form. Emergence highlights energy’s creative aspect, building order and complexity over time.

Energy:
The fundamental essence underlying all forms, processes, and experiences. It manifests as matter, space, and information, and is the force that enables change, sustains existence, and carries meaning through structure and transformation.

Entropy (2nd Law):
A principle describing energy’s tendency to move toward simpler, more uniform states. It represents a “dissolving” force, increasing disorder and reducing structured complexity without external input.

Eros/Agape:
Terms borrowed from philosophical and spiritual traditions describing different dimensions of love or relational energy:

    Eros: A love characterized by desire, attraction, and the creative spark that ignites growth and transformation.
    Agape: A love that is unconditional, universal, and encompassing, aligned with compassion and unity.
    In an energetic context, these complementary forces shape relational patterns from personal interactions to cosmic harmonies.

Evolution/Involution:
Dual forces describing how complexity arises and recedes:

    Evolution: The outward unfolding, diversification, and increasing complexity of forms.
    Involution: The inward turning, concentrating, or integrating movement toward deeper structure.
    Together, they highlight the bidirectional dance of complexity in energetic and informational domains.

First, Second, Third-Person Perspectives (as a Triple):
A three-fold framework to categorize experiential viewpoints:

    First-Person Perspective: The subjective, individual vantage point—personal “I.”
    Second-Person Perspective: The relational vantage point—engagement between “I” and “you.”
    Third-Person Perspective: The objective vantage point—observing phenomena externally.
    This triple aids in understanding how energy and consciousness are interpreted differently depending on the observer’s relational stance.

Holonics:
A perspective that views systems as composed of parts (holons) that are both wholes and parts of larger systems. In energy terms, holonics helps understand interdependent networks and nested systems supporting one another.

Information:
Structured patterns encoded in energy. More than mere data, information here is how energy organizes itself, shaping relationships, guiding systems, and enabling meaning to emerge from complexity.

Matter:
A form of energy in a condensed, tangible state. Matter’s stability and structure give rise to the physical world, providing a substrate for life, technology, and experience.

Metaphysics:
The philosophical inquiry into the fundamental nature of reality, existence, and the principles that underlie the observable universe. In this context, metaphysics provides the conceptual backbone for understanding energy, consciousness, and the interplay of forms.

Omega:
A conceptual endpoint or ultimate purpose of the universe’s evolution. It can be interpreted as a goal or direction in which the cosmic narrative unfolds, potentially culminating in higher consciousness or universal harmony.

Space:
An expansive, relational form of energy, providing the “stage” upon which matter and information unfold. Space is not empty but charged with relationships and potential pathways for energy’s flow and transformation.

Survival/Social/Sexual (as a Triple):
A triad describing core energetic dimensions of life:

    Survival: The energetic imperative to maintain existence.
    Social: The energetic flows governing relationships and cultural evolution.
    Sexual: The generative force driving reproduction and diversification.
    This triple maps fundamental energetic patterns shaping biological and cultural existence.

Sustainable Energy:
Energy sources and systems that meet current needs without compromising future generations. Emphasizing renewability, stability, and minimal ecological harm, sustainable energy aligns with long-term cosmic equilibrium.

Triplication (from Forrest Landry’s Immanent Metaphysics):
A conceptual tool that employs sets of three foundational, interrelated terms to structure metaphysical understanding. Triplication fosters nuance and coherence in philosophical inquiry, ensuring concepts are balanced and contextualized from multiple angles.

Universal Consciousness:
A concept positing that consciousness pervades the entire cosmos, operating as a fundamental characteristic of energy. This universal perspective suggests all phenomena emerge from and contribute to a grand field of awareness.

Witness-Observer Dynamic:
The relationship between the act of observing and the phenomena observed. This dynamic ensures that any form of energy or pattern must be perceived for it to gain meaning, establish difference, and become part of experience.
